Step 2 Selection Phase

Identify Habits, Patterns & Beliefs

Naming what has been running beneath the surface, so it no longer runs you.

The Teaching

In Step 1 you acknowledged the reality you want to move away from. Here, you go one layer deeper: the habits, patterns, and beliefs that have been holding that old reality in place. These are the invisible anchors. Until you name them, they work on you quietly.

Your emotions are information. When something happens in your day and a feeling arises that does not feel good, there is a thought underneath it. That thought is often a belief running on repeat, things like "I always fail at this" or "I am not good enough." Your reality obeys what you tell yourself most consistently.

Awareness is the first act of change. You cannot release what you have not yet seen. This step asks you to look clearly, without judgment, at the three layers that shape your current experience: what you do automatically, what you tell yourself, and the cycles you keep returning to.

Before You Write

Before you begin writing, take one slow breath and place a hand on your chest. Notice any tightness or heaviness that arises as you think about what you are about to name. You do not need to fix anything right now. You are simply becoming a witness to yourself.

Your Reflection

Look at the life you have been living. Which habits have kept you anchored to the reality you named in Step 1, the ones you have maybe even tried to break before, but keep returning to? These do not need to be a long list. Focus on the ones that matter most.

I am no longer a person who...

What does the mind chatter say on repeat? Notice the thoughts that surface when something in your day goes wrong, the quiet verdict you deliver about yourself in those moments. The most important opinion you hold is the one you have of yourself.

The beliefs I am ready to release are...

A pattern is different from a habit. A habit is something you do automatically, while a pattern is a cycle of behavior or relating that carries emotional weight and keeps recurring even when you recognize it. Cycles of relationships, of giving away your power, of self-sabotage at the edge of something good. What patterns are you ready to identify?
